Soft Robotics in Knee Surgery Recovery

The Problem

  • More than 30% of the patients loose leg mobility.
  • 1/4 of the patients neglect physiotherapy due to lack of support.
  • Existing technologies for physiotherapy are heavy, expensive, and not proper for domestic use.

Motion study of the physiotherapy exercise:

The Solution

  • User-friendly and lightweight soft robotics.
  • A triangular cushion to perform a macromovement to exercise the leg.
  • A leg brace to perform a micromovent to prevent blood clots.
  • Flex sensor and EMG sensors to track progress and enable remote guidance from health professional.

Cotton vs Nature - VR documentary

The Problem

Every year, millions of acres of forests are lost to agriculture, contributing to climate change. People are not aware of this impact.

The Solution

I produced a virtual reality documentary in the Chapada dos Guimarães national park to teleport investors to one of the most targeted deforestation spots in Brazil and convince them to support cell agriculture research.

So, I had the idea to go back there to share this personal experience to emotionally touch the investors. We used Insta 360 Pro II, a stereoscopic 360 camera that creates an extremely immersive experience. The video had such an impact on investors that, later, Forbes published the video.

AR Museum - Preserving Indigenous Memories

The Problem

Indigenous culture in Brazil, such as the Tukanos and Tupis', heavily rely on natural elements for its preservation. These tribes use rivers, caves, and forests to tell stories. These sacred places are being affected by deforestation.

The Solution

I curated and web-scraped over 2,000 entries of indigenous stories, legends, rituals, and traditions. Using generative AI, I visualized these stories through compelling imagery and organized them into thematic clusters. To preserve and celebrate these narratives, I developed an app that transforms the world into a living museum. Through its augmented reality feature, users can experience these stories in their natural environments, fostering interaction with the cultural context without displacing artifacts from their original locations.

VR Cell analysis

The Problem

The scientists could not measure the cells that were entangling around each other. A 2D image was not able to analyse overlapping cells and traditional measuring methods would break the artificially elongated cells.

The Solution

I developed a cell 3D scan method, a VR app to visualize and annotate the 3D cell models. The app shrunk the scientists to a cell size and transported people to the microscopic world. The app enabled the fiber analysis from a close distance.



We tested different scanning methods: SEM, Latscan and the confocal microscope. The best results were obtained through the confocal with a proper staining protocol. To recreate the 3D model, I filtered and stacked each layer of the confocal microscope image, obtaining a point cloud.

AI cashier

During college I joined the computational geometry lab where I researched various computer vision solutions. My graduation thesis - presented at INDUSCON and published by IEEE - is an automation project for bakeries and grocery stores, using the YOLOv4 object detector.

Our team developed a self-checkout cashier wherein the client puts a tray with the products underneath a webcam, and the system returns the total price. To achieve this goal, we developed:

Internship at Utokyo - Teeth Scan & Segmentation

FAPESP granted me a scholarship to conduct a research on computer vision. I developed a teeth scan solution that was presented at SIICUSP. Later, I was invited to continue my research at The University of Tokyo (#23 QS ranking, 2019). I studied CG, computational geometry, and mesh segmentation in Japan.

The entire research consisted in 5 steps: teeth mold scan, point cloud processing, surface reconstruction, mesh processing, and segmentation of the teeth. The research was presented at ICGG and the paper was published here.


On the first step, I scanned a negative mold of the teeth. I used three distinct methods: photogrametry (Horus System), a depth camera (Xbox Kinect), and an interferometry scanner. During this step, I studied about camera calibration, point cloud processing, and different scanning methods.

Secondly, my algorithm filtered and concatenated the point cloud. I took diferent scans in different angles in order to avoid any missing detail. ICP - a function that stitches common features from each sample - joined the various point cloud.

Then, I reconstructed the surface using marching cubes and poisson mesh reconstruction. Next, normal vectors in each vertex smoothed the reconstructed mesh and a region growing algorithm based on the curvature threshold is applied to the mesh for segmentation.
