organ-on-a-chip

Tissuelabs is a tissue engineering startup. The companies' dream was to manufacture an artificial transplantable heart. Lucas proposed the first organ-on-a-chip produced by the company using stereolithography 3D printers. Organ-on-a-chip is an organ model used to simulate the activities, mechanics and physiological response of an organ. The model is composed by a 3D microfluidic circuit integrated to a cell culture. Each channel can represent blood vessels and other parts of the human body. For instance, a alveoli model is composed by an air sac and a network of blood vessels around the sac.

Lucas was responsible for designing and testing most of the organ-on-a-chip and microfluidics 3D models at Tissuelabs. He developed more than 50 different models that were used by scientists to conduct experiments and were also used to showcase the equipment capabilities. Some of these models can be seen on the companies' video: video link

Cell analysis

One of the problems the scientists were facing at Galy was to analyse the cotton fibers. They could not measure the cells because they were entangling around each other. A 2D image was not able to analyse overlapping cells. Lucas proposed to analyse a 3D scan of the cells. An extra dimension can provide more information to the analysis. A 3D scan technique was developed to scan the cells. Lucas developed a VR app to visualize and annotate the 3D cell models. The app shrinked the scientists to a cellular level and enabled the fiber analysis from a close distance. The next step would be to create an AI to segmentate and measure the fibers.

AI cashier

Lucas graduation thesis is an automation project for bakeries and grocery stores, using the YOLOv4 object detector. A self-checkout cashier was developed where the client puts a tray with the products underneath a webcam, and the system returns the total price of the purchase. To achieve this goal, it was developed some process and protocols, a user interface, a admin interface, a segmentation algorithm - to automatically segmentate the training data, and a server to integrate it all. Internship at Utokyo - Teeth Scan & Segmentation

FAPESP granted Lucas a scholarship to conduct a research on teeth scan. The research was presented at SIICUSP. Later, Lucas was invited to continue his research at The University of Tokyo (#23 QS ranking, 2019). Lucas studied CG, computational geometry, and mesh segmentation during his time in Japan. The entire research consisted in 5 steps: teeth mold scan, point cloud processing, surface reconstruction, mesh processing, and segmentation of the teeth. The research was presented at ICGG and the paper was published here.

On the first step, Instead of scanning the mouth, Lucas proposed to scan the negative mold. 3 different methods were used to scan the mold. We used a low-cost equipment called horus that uses photogrametry. We used kinect that has a depth camera. We used a interferometry scanner. The interferometry scanner obtained the best result, followed by the horus, and kinect. We concluded that kinect is not suited for scanning small objects. During this step, Lucas studied about camera calibration, point cloud processing and visualization, and different scanning methods.

Secondly, the resulted point cloud was filtered and concatenated. Many diferent scans were performed in different angles in order to avoid missing any detail. To join the various point cloud together an algorithm called ICP was used. The algorithm tries to find common charateristic features in each sample to stitch them together.

Then, the mesh is reconstructed. 2 methods were tested: marching cubes and poisson. The reconstructed mesh is smoothed by calculating the normal vectors. A region growing based on the curvature threshold is applied to the mesh to segmentate the teeth. Some heuristics were assumed in order to improve the accuracy of the algorithm. For instance, we re-positioned the teeth to face upwards by calculating the principal components of the model.
