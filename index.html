Lucas Miyazaki

linha do tempo (com projetos e links) faltou proj hackaton e sociais skills Mechatronics engineer, web developer, and design enthusiast Lucas Kyoshi Miyazaki graduated at the University of Sao Paulo and worked for two biotech companies. His main research field is computational geometry. Why Computational Geometry? In the virtual world, geometry becomes a powerful tool, an instrument that allows us to craft and shape worlds limited only by our imagination. In this digital dimension, everything is possible, and the boundaries of creativity are boundless. When we seamlessly interface the virtual world with our physical reality, we unlock the ability to transform our wildest dreams into tangible experiences. This fusion opens doors to innovation, creativity, and solutions that were once considered unattainable. I am driven by the belief that by harnessing the power of computational geometry, we can reshape our reality and make the seemingly impossible, possible. For instance, people were facing problems to analyse entangled cotton fibers. I created a method to scan the fibers and a VR app to visualize it. It made shrinking the scientists to the cell level possible. In the virtual world is also possible to create organs and blood vessels. These organs can be 3D printed.
